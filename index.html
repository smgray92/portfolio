About Me

I graduated from the University of Washington in 2015 with a Bachelor's of Science Degree in Microbiology. After graduating, I earned a position as a Micorbiologist and Lab Technician at Institue for Environmental Health in Lake Forest Park, WA. In this job, I specialize in Bacterial DNA fingerprinting (also known as PFGE), which visually compares different strains of Bacteria. Other than this job position, I have previously worked as a Barista in one of the cafes of the Univeristy, and a Lab Technician in a Protein Crystallography Laboratory in the University. Although I appreciate Biology and working with microorganisms, I would like to be in a field that is continually growing and is not always repetitive. This is how I've taken an interest in programming, and I also have an optimistic view of applying Biology with Computer Science so that I can appreciate my original field of interest as well.
